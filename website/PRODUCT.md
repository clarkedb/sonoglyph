# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Fellow tinkerers: hobbyists, hackers, and makers who share the project's _Project Hail Mary_ / radio / maker sensibility. They arrive curious, not evaluating — kindred spirits rather than customers. A visitor's context is leisure-time exploration: following a link from a repo, a forum, or a friend, wondering "what is this thing?"

## Product Purpose

The public face of Sonoglyph: project introduction, hosted playground, and the Learn section (interactive articles grown from the playground's embedded explainers). Success is the moment it clicks — a visitor opens the playground, presses a key, watches the signal flow through every stage of the pipeline, and walks away understanding something about signal processing they didn't before.

## Positioning

Any signal system, one pipeline: a plugin architecture that turns any structured signal — DTMF, Morse, chords, alien languages — into glyphs.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: open the playground. Secondary: the GitHub repo.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: **watch sound become symbols**.
- Belief ladder: (1) decoding signals is genuinely cool → (2) I could actually understand how this works → (3) trying costs nothing — it runs in my browser right now.
- Proof on hand: the playground itself — a real, working, inspectable pipeline is the entire proof. No testimonials, no logos; the demo is the argument.

## Brand Personality

Nerdy, cinematic, wondrous. _Project Hail Mary_ energy: hard science with awe — decoding an alien language at 2am. The voice is a maker showing you something amazing they built, not a company selling you something.

Reference feel: vintage instrument manuals mixed with space — dark-mode-first, graph-paper spec-sheet nerdiness. HP/Tektronix diagram soul crossed with the void; technical drawings that evoke wonder.

## Anti-references

Not a SaaS landing page: no hero metric, no gradient CTAs, no testimonial wall, no pricing-page grammar. Nothing is being sold.

## Design Principles

1. **The demo is the argument.** Show the pipeline running; never describe what could be shown. Every claim on the page should be one click from proof.
2. **Wonder first, rigor beneath.** Lead with the cinematic moment (sound becoming symbols); let the engineering exactness reveal itself on inspection — like a good instrument.
3. **Instrument, not brochure.** The site reads like a beautifully drafted spec sheet for a machine you can actually operate — measurements, diagrams, annotations — not like marketing.
4. **Teaching is the product.** Every visual doubles as an explanation; decoration that doesn't explain something is suspect.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast throughout. Motion care is first-class: every animation ships a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback (the cinematic ambitions make this non-negotiable). Color is never the sole signal carrier — spectra, waveforms, and glyph states stay readable for colorblind users.
